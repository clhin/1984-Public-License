# 1984-Public-License Version 1.0 (Or 1984PL for short)
An Anti Authoritarian Free Software License

## What is this?
This is my attempt at curbing the wave of legislation impacting the free software community.

## What is the motivation behind this?
In the 1990s and 2000s, the biggest wave of threats to free software was patents and hardware locking. As time has passed, the most recent threat has come from legislators who know little to nothing about what their legislation is doing. For instance with age verifiction. Many legislators have no idea that operating systems are in everything from computers to servers to cell phone modems to pacemakers. They wave their pens around and try to dictate what people can and cannot write and often do not pay attention nor care about the consequences. They write these bills, and yet their public infrastructure takes advantage of the free software that they make people's lives harder to write. I will not permit this any longer. The Free Software Foundation has repeatedly failed to meet the moment to combat these threats, so I took matters into my own hands.

## So what does it do?
In short, the license states that if a government body tries to legislate what a piece of software can or cannot say, or tries to compel code, any government agency, governing body, or individual associated with the government, has their license rendered null and void. Furthermore the user agrees if they try to legislate against the software, they can have their code remotely removed from their device. It is a viral copyleft license, and it goes a step further: any LLM that scrapes this code for their model must generate code under this license.

## Can you really do that? What about malware and whatnot?
Yes. Code is free speech under the US constitution. You cannot compel speech. You can however protect against the outcome of the speech. If someone wants to write a computer worm, they should be within their rights to do so, they could be using it for security testing for instance. However if they go and release that worm, that of course is a reasonable thing to legislate against. Legislate against the effect, not the code itself.

## Doesn't this violate FSF and OSI license guidelines?
No, I don't believe this license violates free software legal standings any more than the GPL's patent restrictions. Patent holders used their patents as a cudgel against free software writers while taking advantage of their work. My license dictates the same thing, but against legislators. If you are a lawyer and disagree with me however, feel free to write an issue or a pull request and we can chat about it.
